VIRAT KOHLI

The man who Ruled World Cricket

Here's a time line of Virat Kohli's life:

  • 1988 - Born on November 5th in Delhi, India
  • 1998 - Joins the West Delhi Cricket Academy and starts training in cricket at a young age
  • 2006 - Captains the Indian U-19 cricket team to victory in the U-19 Cricket World Cup held in Sri Lanka
  • 2008 - Makes his debut for the Indian national cricket team in August against Sri Lanka in an One Day International (ODI) match
  • 2010 - Scores his first ODI century against Sri Lanka and becomes the fastest Indian to reach 1,000 runs in ODIs
  • 2011 - Plays a crucial role in India's victory in the ICC Cricket World Cup, scoring important runs throughout the tournament
  • 2012 - Becomes the captain of the Indian cricket team for the first time during the Asia Cup, in the absence of MS Dhoni
  • 2013 - Appointed as the vice-captain of the Indian Test team for the tour of South Africa
  • 2014 - Kohli's stellar performances in all formats of the game lead him to be appointed as the captain of the Indian Test team after MS Dhoni's retirement from Test cricket
  • 2016 - Named as the captain of the Indian limited-overs teams (ODI and T20I) after MS Dhoni steps down from the captaincy
  • 2017 - Registers his highest individual Test score of 243 runs against Sri Lanka in a Test match
  • 2018 - Becomes the fastest player to reach 10,000 runs in One Day Internationals during a match against West Indies
  • 2019 - Leads India to its first-ever Test series victory in Australia as captain
  • 2020 - Named as the ICC Men's ODI Cricketer of the Decade for his extraordinary performances in the 2010s
  • 2021 - Steps down as the captain of the T20I team after the T20 World Cup, but continues to lead the Test and ODI teams
  • 2023 - Announces his retirement from all formats of international cricket, leaving behind a remarkable legacy as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of the game
